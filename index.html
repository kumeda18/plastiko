A Brief
History of Plastic
1933
Polyethylene, the most commonly used plastic, is accident at a chemical plant in Northwich, England.
1965
The one-piece polyethylene shopping bag is designed by Sten Gustaf Thulin, a Swedish  engineer.

The bag is patented by the Swedish company Celloplast that same year.
1982
Safeway and Kroger, two of the biggest supermarket chains in the United States, switch to plastic bags.

More stores follow suit and by the end of the decade plastic bags will have almost replaced paper around the world.
1997
Sailor and researcher Charles Moore discovers the Great Pacific Garbage Patch, the largest of several gyres in the world’s oceans
2020
This year around 5 trillion plastic bags were used.

That breaks down to 160,000 plastic bags used per second.

By 2050, the world's oceans could contain more plastic than fish measured by weight.

Legislation
Bangladesh
In, 2002, Bangladesh became the first country in the world to implement a ban on thin plastic bags.
‍
Plastics clogged their drainage systems used during disastrous flooding.
Other Countries Followed

Countries with 1st Generatin/straight bans include:
Belgium, Albania, Kenya, Chile, Fiji, Georgia, Haiti, Italy, Jamaica, Madagascar, Mali, Morroco, New Zealand, Nigeria, Pakistan, South Korea, Vatican City, Tunisia, Sri Lanka, Papua New Guinea.....
AND MORE
The USA

California was the 1st state with a uniform statewide plastic bag reduction law.

Other States that have followed: OR, NY, VT, ME, CT, DE
Do these laws even work?
After California passed its statewide ban, reusable bag use increased by 58%.
Plastic bag litter decreased by 59% on streets and 89% in storm drains
